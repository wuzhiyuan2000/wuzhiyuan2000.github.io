About Me

         I'm currently a master’s candidate and a research assistant with Institute of Computing Technology, Chinese Academy of Sciences. Before that, I was an research intern at Institute of Software Systems and Engineering, Tsinghua University, from Feb. 2021 to Jun. 2021, and at the Key Laboratory of Symbolic Computation and Knowledge Engineering of Ministry of Education, Jilin University, from Jun. 2020 to Oct. 2021.

I am a Member of the China Computer Federation (CCF).

Research Interests

My research interests include some key technologies related to Edge Intelligence and Machine Learning:

  • Mobile Computing is human-computer interaction in which a computer is expected to be transported during normal usage, which allows for the transmission of data, voice, and video.

  • Federated Learning is a distributed machine learning paradigm that trains an algorithm across multiple decentralized edge devices holding local data samples, without exchanging them.

  • Knowledge Distillation is a machine learning technique that transfers the knowledge from one machine learning model to another, enabling heterogeneous machine learning models learn from each other to realize constructively optimization.

Selected Publications

Representative Publications

Exploring the Distributed Knowledge Congruence in Proxy-data-free Federated Distillation.

Zhiyuan Wu, et al. Preprint.

The First Work to Consider Knowledge Incongruence Among Heterogeneous Clients in Proxy-data-free Federated Distillation. Propose a communication-efficient and accuracy guaranteed FD algorithm, FedDKC, with knowledge refinement strategies KKR and SKR. With our method, knowledge discrepancy between arbitrating clients satisfies an acceptable upper bound is theoretically proved. Work closely related to model-heterogeneous federated learning and knowledge distillation.
