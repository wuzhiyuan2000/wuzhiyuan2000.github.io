Research Interests

My research interests include some key technologies related to Edge Intelligence and Machine Learning:

  • Mobile Computing is human-computer interaction in which a computer is expected to be transported during normal usage, which allows for the transmission of data, voice, and video.

  • Federated Learning is a distributed machine learning paradigm that trains an algorithm across multiple decentralized edge devices without exchanging local data samples on devices.

  • Knowledge Distillation is a machine learning technique that transfers the knowledge from one machine learning model to another, enabling heterogeneous machine learning models learn from each other to realize constructively optimization.

  • Distributed Optimization studies to optimize a certain objective defined over millions of billions of data that is distributed over many machines by utilizing the computational power of these machines.

Selected Publications

Representative Publications

FedICT: Federated Multi-task Distillation for Multi-access Edge Computing.

Zhiyuan Wu, et al. Preprint.

Propose a distillation-based federated multi-task learning algorithm, FedICT, with FPKD for personalizing client-side distillation and LKA for correcting server-side distillation. FedICT enables efficient communication and heterogeneous models without the assistance of a public dataset, and can be deemed as the First Federated Distillation Algorithm on Multi-task Setting to be Practically Deployed in Multi-access Edge Computing. Work closely related to mobile computing, federated learning and knowledge distillation.

Exploring the Distributed Knowledge Congruence in Proxy-data-free Federated Distillation.

Zhiyuan Wu, et al. Preprint.

The First Work to Consider Knowledge Incongruence Among Heterogeneous Clients in Proxy-data-free Federated Distillation. Propose a communication-efficient and accuracy guaranteed federated distillation algorithm, FedDKC, with knowledge refinement strategies KKR and SKR. With our method, knowledge discrepancy between arbitrating clients satisfies an acceptable upper bound is theoretically proved. Work closely related to federated learning and knowledge distillation.
